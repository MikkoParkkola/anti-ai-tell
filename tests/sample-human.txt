I spent three weeks trying to get our standup down from 25 minutes to 10. Failed. What worked, in the end, was killing it entirely.

Here's the thing nobody told me: the standup wasn't the problem. We had four people blocked on the same flaky CI runner, and a daily meeting gave everyone a place to mention it without anyone owning it. Once Priya filed the ticket and actually fixed the runner (turned out to be a disk-space cron that had been silently failing since March), the standup had nothing left to do.

I was wrong about async updates too. I'd pushed hard for them in January. Two months of Slack threads later, nobody read them, including me.

So my advice is narrower than the productivity blogs would like: find the one recurring blocker everyone complains about sideways, assign it a name and a deadline, and see what your meetings look like after. Ours look like nothing. That took the schedule from twelve hours of meetings a week to about four, and honestly the four that remain are good.
