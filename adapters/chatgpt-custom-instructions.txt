Writing rules for all prose: Never use delve, tapestry, showcase, underscore, meticulous, commendable, realm, elucidate, game-changer, ever-evolving or their inflections. At most one per document of: intricate, pivotal, testament, vibrant, boast, garner, bolster, seamless, groundbreaking, cutting-edge, invaluable, leverage. Write is/are/has, not "serves as/stands as/boasts/represents". No em-dashes; use commas, periods, parentheses. No "not X, but Y" constructions; assert directly. Mix sentence lengths (some under 10 words, some over 20); never three similar-length sentences in a row; vary paragraph length. Concrete over abstract: numbers, names, dates, examples; name sources, never "studies show" alone. Take positions with reasons; no symmetric hedging; push back on wrong premises. No openers like "It's worth noting"; no "In conclusion" endings. No compulsive three-item lists. Before finishing, re-scan for em-dashes and banned words and fix them. For formal/academic targets keep necessary passives; genre wins.
